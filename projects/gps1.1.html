What is pair programming?

The process of two people working simultaneously on a piece of code to fix a bug or develop a program. There are usually 2 roles: the driver and the navigator.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programming is important for a few reasons:

  1. It prepares you for the marketplace/realworld. You will have to collaborate with other programmers in work, and practicing this process in DBC makes you better at it!
  2. Develops interpersonal and teamwork skills. You have to communicate ideas and explain concepts to another person while working for a common goal, making you a more collaborative person.
  3. Create more complete solutions. Two brains working on one problem is better than one.
Pair Programming Roles
Driver Navigator
The driver is the person with the key. They are at the keyboard and actually creating the code. The navigator is the person who guides the driver through the problem. They have space to think about the big picture while the driver works on the details.
